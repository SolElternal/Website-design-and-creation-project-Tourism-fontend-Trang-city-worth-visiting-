Koh Libong

Highlights of Koh Libong

From the island, there is a bridge extending out to the sea, approximately 1 kilometer long, which is a walkway for tourists to the "Aquatic Animal Watching Tower" or the Dugong Watching Tower, which is a 5-story tower. During high tide, you may be able to see Dugongs come to eat sea grass in this area and it is also a point where you can see beautiful scenery around you. You can also island. which surrounds Koh Libong Filled with sea grass The main food plant for the dugong, a mammal. For travelers who like adventure Another interesting recommended activity is Shooting up the mountain

Of course, Koh Libong is the source of dugong food. There must be a lot of dugongs living there. Here you can find the largest flock of dugongs in Thailand. The villagers also join together to preserve dugongs from extinction. We can go see the dugongs at The aquatic life observation tower (Dugong viewing tower) is located at the end of Leek Phai Bridge. Or another place is Mount Batu Puteh This point is higher than the aquarium viewing tower. Can see more clearly.

About of Koh Libong

You can travel to Koh Libong by taking a boat from. Hat Yao Pier To Koh Libong Pier There will be a boat available from 8:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m. It takes about 30 minutes to reach the island. As for traveling on the island, You can rent a motorcycle, trailer or car to travel on the island. By that fare I will think according to the location.

In addition, there are many places to visit, including Hat Tub, a beach behind the mountain, and a freshwater pond in the middle of the sea.
